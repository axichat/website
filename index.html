FAQ

Does Axichat provide real email addresses @axi.im?

Yes, use it as you would any other email address, including to sign up for other online accounts. Your chat address (JID) and email address are exactly the same.

Do I need an existing email address to sign up?

No. Create your account on this website with a username and password, then add a recovery method if you have one available.

Can I still receive email from my previous address?

Yes, forward emails from your existing provider to your new "@axi.im" address. If you use Gmail or Outlook, here are some quick links: Gmail, Outlook.

Is it FOSS?

Yes, Axichat is free and open source software as you can see on GitHub and GitLab.

Can I self-host?

Yes, read the guide on using your own server with the Axichat client.

If Axichat uses XMPP, is it federated?

Yes, XMPP is used for messaging other Axichat users and anyone with a valid JID.

How does Axichat compare to Spike?

Both Spike and Axichat are tremendous improvements over traditional email clients. However,

  • Axichat provides a richer IM experience by using a combination of chat (XMPP) and email (SMTP) protocols. Spike limits itself to SMTP.
  • Spike is more targeted to business teams, whereas Axichat works just as well for individual, family, and business use cases.
  • Axichat is open source, so you can see our code.
  • Axichat was made with Flutter, which provides significantly better UI and performance on all platforms.
What if I don't like Axichat?

Request the features you want or you can export all your messages and contacts.

If you want to unregister entirely, open your profile page in the app and go to Account > Unregister.
